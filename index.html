What is D.E.A.R. M.A.N.?

D.E.A.R. M.A.N. is a skill coming from Dialectical Behavioral Therapy, or DBT. It is used to have a more successful interaction with someone when asking for something or saying no to something. D.E.A.R. M.A.N. is an acronym used for remembering the steps in this process.

It stands for:

  1. Describe
  2. Express
  3. Assert
  4. Reinforce
  5. Mindful
  6. Appear Confident
  7. Negotiate

These all break down into a script one can follow for more successful interactions. Each part of D.E.A.R. M.A.N. is explained below and used to create a script to practice before an upcoming interaction.

D.E.A.R. M.A.N. can be used to either ask for something, or to say no to something. To adjust the examples used below, choose the proper option for your situation below:

Describe

The first step is to describe the problem only using facts. It is important to be specific and clear in describing the topic. This step may not be necessary if the situation has already been described (i.e. if you are saying no to a request that was just asked).

Stay Mindful

It is important during the conversation to stay on track. If the other party responds with agression or defensivness, do not engage with it. Rather, continue stating your request or no like a broken record. Getting off topic will reduce the likelyhood of your goal being met.

Appear Confident

During the conversation, keep a strong posture (stand/sit tall), a strong tone, and keep stammering and uncertainty to a minimum. This makes it more likely for the other party to take your request seriously, and it makes you harder to turn down.

Negotiate

In some cases, it is important to give a little to get a little. If the other party isn't in agreeance with your request or no, it can be useful to try to find an alternate solution to the issue that aligns with your goals. You could eventually ask the other party to offer a solution that would work. Negotiation can also serve to make future interactions with the other party easier.
